# Forwarding & Validation

Bob has peeled his layer and extracted his forwarding instructions. He knows the amount to forward, the outgoing CLTV, and the channel to use. But he can't just blindly forward the payment. First, he needs to validate that the economics make sense.

## What Bob Has After Peeling

After `peel_layer`, Bob has two things:

1. **His TLV payload**: The forwarding instructions that Alice encrypted for him.
   - `amt_to_forward`: 50,003,000 msat (how much to send to Carol)
   - `outgoing_cltv_value`: 700,048 (CLTV expiry for the outgoing HTLC)
   - `short_channel_id`: 700000x2x0 (which channel to forward on)

2. **The next onion packet**: A fresh 1,366-byte packet to include in the `update_add_htlc` message to Carol.

But Bob also knows the **incoming** HTLC details from Alice's `update_add_htlc`:
- Incoming amount: 50,009,000 msat (what Alice is offering to pay Bob)
- Incoming CLTV: 700,088 (Alice's timelock)

The gap between incoming and outgoing is Bob's **fee** and **timelock margin**. Bob needs to verify that both are sufficient.

## The Validation Checks

Before forwarding, Bob performs four checks:

<validation-checks></validation-checks>

**Check 1: Fee sufficiency.** Is the incoming amount large enough to cover the forwarded amount plus Bob's fee? For Bob: `expected_fee = 1,000 + floor(50,003,000 * 100 / 1,000,000) = 1,000 + 5,000 = 6,000 msat`. The incoming amount is 50,009,000 and `amt_to_forward + fee = 50,003,000 + 6,000 = 50,009,000`. Exactly right.

**Check 2: Timelock safety.** Is the incoming CLTV high enough to give Bob a safe margin? For Bob: `700,088 >= 700,048 + 40 = 700,088`. Exactly right.

**Check 3: Not expired.** Is the outgoing CLTV still in the future? If the outgoing CLTV has already passed, Bob would be creating an HTLC that's already claimable, which is dangerous.

**Check 4: Channel exists with sufficient liquidity.** Does Bob actually have a channel matching `short_channel_id` with enough capacity to forward the payment? (We'll skip this check in the exercise since we're focused on the cryptographic protocol, but real nodes always check.)

<details>
  <summary>What happens if Bob forwards with insufficient fee?</summary>

If Bob forwards less than Carol expects, Carol will reject the HTLC when she validates her own forwarding instructions. Carol's onion payload tells her to forward 50,000,000 msat to Dave, and Carol's fee policy requires `500 + floor(50,000,000 * 50 / 1,000,000) = 3,000 msat`. If the incoming amount from Bob is less than 50,003,000, Carol will reject with an `incorrect_or_unknown_payment_details` error. This error propagates back through the onion as an encrypted error message (which we'll cover in a later section).
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why does Bob care about the timelock margin?</summary>

The timelock margin protects Bob from a timing attack. Suppose Alice offers Bob a CLTV of 700,050 and Bob's outgoing CLTV to Carol is 700,048. That's only 2 blocks of margin. If the blockchain advances 2 blocks while Carol is processing the payment, Bob's incoming HTLC could expire before Carol's outgoing HTLC. Carol could claim the payment (via the preimage), but Bob couldn't claim from Alice because his HTLC has already expired. Bob's `cltv_expiry_delta` (40 blocks in our canonical trace) ensures he always has a safe buffer.
</details>

## Putting It All Together: End-to-End

We now have both sides of the Sphinx protocol: `SphinxPacketBuilder` (Alice's side) and `SphinxPacketProcessor` (Bob, Carol, and Dave's side). The ultimate test is to connect them.

Alice builds a packet with `SphinxPacketBuilder`. Bob peels one layer with `SphinxPacketProcessor`. Carol peels the next layer. Dave peels the final layer. At each hop, the peeled payload should exactly match the forwarding instructions that Alice originally encoded.

This end-to-end property is what makes Sphinx work:
- **Bob** sees `amt_to_forward = 50,003,000`, `outgoing_cltv = 700,048`, `short_channel_id = 700000x2x0`
- **Carol** sees `amt_to_forward = 50,000,000`, `outgoing_cltv = 700,018`, `short_channel_id = 700000x3x0`
- **Dave** sees `amt_to_forward = 50,000,000`, `outgoing_cltv = 700,018`, no `short_channel_id` (final hop)
- **Dave's** `is_final` flag is `True` (all-zero HMAC)

If any of these values are wrong, either the builder or the processor has a bug. If the HMAC fails at any hop, something was tampered with or computed incorrectly. The end-to-end test verifies the entire chain.
