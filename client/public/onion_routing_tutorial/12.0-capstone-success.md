# Capstone: Successful Payment

You've made it! Over the last eleven chapters, you wrote every function a Sphinx onion needs: the shared-secret chain, the key derivation, the filler, the wrap, the peel, the HMAC verification, and the forwarding checks. Now comes the payoff. In this capstone, your code (yes, the exact code you wrote in the exercises) runs a complete Lightning payment from Alice to Dave, and you get to step through every line of it, debugger-style.

## What happens when you click "Run the capstone"

1. **Preflight.** The lab re-runs all nine exercise test suites against your saved code. The capstone only unlocks once every suite passes. If something fails, the panel lists exactly which exercise to revisit, so there's no mystery debugging.
2. **Assembly.** Your builder methods (`derive_shared_secrets`, `generate_filler`, `wrap_hop`, `build`) are stitched into `sphinx/builder.py`, and your forwarder functions (`verify_hmac`, `peel_layer`, `check_forward`) into `sphinx/forwarder.py`. These are the two "files" you'll see in the code pane.
3. **The run.** A fixed route (Alice → Bob → Charlie → Dave, paying Dave 1,000,000 msat) runs entirely in your browser. Alice builds the 1,366-byte onion with your `build`. Then each forwarder verifies, peels, and policy-checks the packet with your functions, until Dave finds the type-8 `payment_data` in his hop payload and the payment lands.

The lab records every step of that run. Once it finishes, you're in the driver's seat... step forward, step back, jump around, and watch the onion change exactly when your code changes it.

## How to drive it

- **The stage** (top) plays out the run as a sequence of scenes, using the same visuals you've seen all course: the 1,300-byte buffer with its hop payloads, the encryption hatches stacking and peeling, the keystream bars, the 1,366-byte packet strip, and the fee and CLTV checks. The caption under the stage narrates what your code is doing at that exact moment.
- **The route strip** doubles as navigation. Click any function chip under any hop (say, `wrap_hop` under Alice, or `peel` under Charlie) to jump straight to that part of the run.
- **The code pane** highlights the line about to execute, in *your* file, with your variable names. Next to it, the variables panel pins the values that matter for the current scene under "This step", with the full Locals and Globals tree below.
- **The controls**: Back, Step, and Step Out move line by line, the Scene buttons hop between beats, and the scrubber (with a tick at every scene boundary) lets you fly across the whole run.

> Note, you step through each function line by line the **first** time it runs. When the same function runs again later in the route (Charlie's peel, for example), the lab shows it as a quick beat with a reminder that you already watched that code work. No reruns, no filler... well, except the filler you wrote in chapter 7.

## Why this is the capstone

This is the moment everything connects. The packet Alice builds in this run is the real thing: back in chapter 8, your `build` already reproduced the official BOLT 4 test vector **byte for byte**, the exact packet that LND, Core Lightning, and LDK all construct from the same inputs. So when you watch Bob's HMAC check pass here, you're not watching a toy. You're watching your code follow the same rules every Lightning node on the planet follows. Pretty cool, right?

And unlike every exercise before it, nothing here is checked in isolation. Your builder feeds your forwarder. Your filler makes Charlie's HMAC verify. Your blinding chain hands each hop the right ephemeral key. One payment, end to end, all yours.
