## The Complete Payment Lifecycle

Let's walk through what happens when Alice pays Dave 50,000 sats (50,000,000 msat) through the route Alice -> Bob -> Carol -> Dave.

### Step 1: Dave Creates an Invoice

Dave generates a payment preimage (a secret only he knows) and hashes it to produce the **payment hash**. He encodes the payment hash, the amount (50,000 sats), and his minimum CLTV expiry delta (18 blocks) into a BOLT 11 invoice and shares it with Alice.

### Step 2: Alice Finds a Route and Calculates Fees

Alice uses her knowledge of the network graph (from gossip) to find a path: Alice -> Bob -> Carol -> Dave. She calculates fees and CLTVs **backward** from Dave:

| Hop | amt_to_forward | outgoing_cltv | Fee earned |
|-----|---------------|---------------|------------|
| **Bob** | 50,003,000 msat | 700,048 | 6,000 msat |
| **Carol** | 50,000,000 msat | 700,018 | 3,000 msat |
| **Dave** | 50,000,000 msat | 700,018 | 0 (recipient) |

Alice will send Bob 50,009,000 msat with CLTV 700,088.

### Step 3: Alice Constructs the Onion Packet

Using `SphinxPacketBuilder`, Alice:
1. Generates a fresh session key (ephemeral, unlinkable to her identity)
2. Computes shared secrets with each hop via ECDH + blinding
3. Derives per-hop keys (rho, mu, um, pad, ammag) from each shared secret
4. Encodes TLV payloads with forwarding instructions for each hop
5. Generates filler bytes to maintain the fixed packet size
6. Wraps layers from innermost (Dave) to outermost (Bob)

The result is a 1,366-byte onion packet: 1 byte version + 33 bytes ephemeral public key + 1,300 bytes encrypted routing info + 32 bytes HMAC.

### Step 4: Alice Sends to Bob

Alice sends `update_add_htlc` to Bob containing:
- The 1,366-byte onion packet
- The payment hash
- Amount: 50,009,000 msat
- CLTV expiry: 700,088

They complete the commitment dance (commitment_signed + revoke_and_ack in both directions) to irrevocably commit the HTLC.

### Step 5: Bob Peels and Forwards

Bob's `SphinxPacketProcessor`:
1. Parses the packet: version, ephemeral key, routing info, HMAC
2. Computes the shared secret using his private key and the ephemeral key
3. Verifies the HMAC (if it fails, Bob sends back an `invalid_onion_hmac` error)
4. Decrypts the routing info with ChaCha20 (using the rho key)
5. Extracts his TLV payload: forward 50,003,000 msat to Carol via channel 700000x2x0, outgoing CLTV 700,048
6. Validates the forward: fee sufficient? CLTV margin safe? Not expired?
7. Re-blinds the ephemeral key and assembles the next 1,366-byte packet for Carol

Bob sends `update_add_htlc` to Carol with the new onion packet, amount 50,003,000 msat, and CLTV 700,048.

### Step 6: Carol Peels and Forwards

Carol performs the same steps as Bob. Her payload says: forward 50,000,000 msat to Dave via channel 700000x3x0, outgoing CLTV 700,018. She validates, re-blinds, and forwards to Dave.

### Step 7: Dave Peels (Final Hop)

Dave peels his layer and finds the **zero HMAC** (32 bytes of zeros) in the next-HMAC position. This signals that he is the final recipient. His payload contains the payment amount (50,000,000 msat) and CLTV (700,018), with no short_channel_id (since there's nowhere to forward).

Dave checks the payment hash against his stored preimage. It matches.

### Step 8: Settlement (Backward)

Dave reveals the **preimage** to Carol via `update_fulfill_htlc`. Carol now has proof of payment and reveals the preimage to Bob. Bob reveals it to Alice.

At each step, the commitment dance secures the state transition. The HTLC is resolved and the forwarding fee is earned.

### Step 9: Alice Has the Preimage

Alice receives the preimage from Bob. She can verify it by hashing it and comparing with the payment hash. The payment is complete. Alice has cryptographic proof that Dave received the funds.

## What If Something Goes Wrong?

Imagine Carol's fee policy changed after Alice computed the route. Carol now requires a higher base fee. When Carol validates the forward, she finds the fee insufficient and generates an error:

1. Carol constructs a 288-byte error packet using `construct_error` with failure code `0x100C` (`fee_insufficient`), encrypted with her shared secret's um and ammag keys
2. Carol sends the error back to Bob
3. Bob wraps the error with another layer of XOR obfuscation using his ammag key
4. Bob sends the wrapped error back to Alice
5. Alice iterates through her shared secrets, removing obfuscation layers one at a time
6. When she removes Bob's layer and then Carol's layer, the HMAC matches, revealing Carol as the source and `0x100C` as the failure code
7. Alice updates her routing graph with Carol's new fee policy and retries through a different route (or the same route with corrected fees)

The error never reveals the full route to any intermediate node. Bob knows an error came from downstream but not specifically from Carol. And the fixed 288-byte size prevents any node from inferring the error's origin by measuring packet size.

## The Capstone Exercise

Your task is to write a `trace_payment` function that orchestrates the complete forward path of this payment. Given a session key, route public keys, route private keys, and hop data, your function will:

1. **Build** the onion packet using `SphinxPacketBuilder`
2. **Peel** at each hop using `SphinxPacketProcessor`
3. **Parse** each hop's TLV payload to extract the forwarding instructions
4. **Verify** that the chain works end-to-end
5. **Return** a structured summary of the trace
